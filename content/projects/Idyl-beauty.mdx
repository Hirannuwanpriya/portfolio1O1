## Overview

IDYL Natural Beauty Salon is a Brussels-based natural beauty and wellness brand
operating across two salon locations — Châtelain/Bailli in Saint-Gilles and
Mérode in Woluwé-Saint-Lambert. The brand focuses on natural, eco-conscious
treatments and positions itself against the conventional beauty industry with
honest, unretouched imagery and sustainably sourced products.

The client needed a complete digital presence that reflected this premium,
values-led brand — a fast, polished website they could fully manage themselves,
in three languages, without ever needing to call a developer.

## The Problem

- The existing online presence didn't reflect the quality and values of the brand.
- All content updates required developer involvement, creating a bottleneck for
  a fast-moving salon business with frequent new treatments, promotions, and
  blog posts.
- The site was French-only, excluding a significant portion of Brussels'
  English- and Dutch-speaking clientele.
- Gift vouchers were being sold in-salon only, leaving online revenue on the table.
- Booking was managed by phone, with no integration to an online booking system.

## My Role

Sole engineer responsible for the full project lifecycle — discovery, architecture,
design implementation, front-end and back-end development, CMS build, e-commerce
integration, third-party booking system connection, deployment, and client
handover and training.

## Solution

### Architecture

Laravel back end serving a Vue.js front end, with all content and translations
stored in a MySQL database and managed through a custom admin panel. Media assets
— hero images, treatment photos, blog images — are stored on AWS S3 and served
via Cloudflare CDN for fast load times across Belgium and neighbouring regions.

### Multilingual Content System

The site is fully trilingual — French, English, and Dutch — with a language
switcher in the header. Every piece of content on every page has a translation
record: page headings, treatment descriptions, blog posts, FAQ entries, team
bios, and navigation labels. The client manages all three languages through the
same CMS interface with a simple language tab per content item.

### Custom CMS with Inline Front-End Editing

The standout feature of the project is the inline editing system. Rather than
asking the client to navigate a separate admin panel to update a heading or swap
a hero image, the CMS exposes an editing toolbar directly on the live website
when the client is authenticated. Clicking any piece of editable content — a
heading, body paragraph, image, or button label — opens an inline editor in
place, and saving pushes the change to the database immediately. The client's
team took to the interface after a single training session and now manages all
content independently.

### Key Features

- Full trilingual site: French, English, and Dutch with per-language URL routing
- Inline front-end editing — content updated directly on the live page without
  navigating to a separate CMS
- Blog / News section with category tagging and multilingual post support
- Gift voucher e-commerce store with online purchase, PDF delivery, and
  multi-use voucher tracking across both salon locations
- Salonkee booking integration for real-time appointment scheduling at both
  Châtelain and Mérode
- Treatments catalogue with filterable categories and individual service detail pages
- Team profiles page with individual therapist bios
- FAQ section managed entirely from the CMS
- Two-location salon directory with contact details, maps, and location-specific
  booking links
- AWS S3 media library with drag-and-drop image upload in the CMS

## Outcomes

- The client's team manages 99% of content updates independently — new blog
  posts, treatment descriptions, promotions, and seasonal hero images — without
  any developer involvement.
- Gift vouchers are now available for online purchase, adding a revenue channel
  that previously required an in-person salon visit.
- The trilingual setup serves Brussels' French-, English-, and Dutch-speaking
  audiences from a single codebase with no duplicate maintenance overhead.
- Online booking via the Salonkee integration reduced phone enquiries and
  improved appointment fill rates across both locations.

## Tech Stack Detail

Vue.js was chosen for the front end to support the reactive inline editing
experience — binding live content fields to the page without full page reloads
made the editing flow feel immediate and approachable for a non-technical client.
Alpine.js was used for lightweight interactive components such as the mobile
navigation, FAQ accordions, and language switcher, keeping JavaScript payload
small for pages that didn't need full Vue reactivity.

AWS S3 was selected for media storage to decouple asset management from the
application server, keeping the Forge-managed Laravel instance lean and making
it straightforward to serve images through Cloudflare's CDN with cache headers
optimised for Core Web Vitals.

## Challenges and Learnings

The trickiest part of the project was designing the inline editing system to be
intuitive for a non-technical user while keeping it architecturally clean. The
initial approach of attaching Vue component instances directly to every editable
DOM node became unwieldy as the number of editable regions grew. The final
solution introduced a single centralised EditableContent component that accepted
a content key and locale prop — any text or image on the site could be made
editable by wrapping it with this component and pointing it at a database key.
This meant the CMS logic lived in one place, and adding new editable regions to
future pages required only a one-line wrapper rather than a new component.

The multilingual gift voucher checkout required careful thought around voucher
redemption UX — the client wanted a single voucher to be usable across both
salon locations and spendable in multiple visits. This was solved with a
`voucher_transactions` ledger table that records each partial redemption against
the voucher balance, rather than marking a voucher as used on first redemption.
