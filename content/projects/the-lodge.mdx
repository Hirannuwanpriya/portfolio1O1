## Overview

The Lodge is one of south-east Brussels' most distinctive hospitality venues —
a restaurant and bar-club that has been welcoming diners, drinkers, and event
guests since 2008, founded by two childhood friends, Hubert and Alexandre, at
Rue des Pêcheries 2 in Watermael-Boitsfort. The venue occupies a uniquely
charming setting: a green enclave with wooden terraces and a glazed veranda
overlooking a private pond, a short distance from Auderghem, Ixelles, and the
entrance to the Namur motorway.

The Lodge operates across three distinct revenue activities: a restaurant serving
Franco-Belgian brasserie cuisine with seasonal Mediterranean and Asian
influences; an upstairs bar-club with a musical atmosphere for evening drinks
and late-night socialising; and a private events business handling birthdays,
corporate team building, and bespoke privatisations with all-in catering packages.

The brief was to build a website that did justice to the venue's character and
atmosphere, communicated all three activities clearly, and gave visitors a direct
path to reserve a table or enquire about a private event.

## The Problem

- A well-established venue with a loyal following and consistently strong reviews
  had no website that matched the warmth and character of the physical experience.
- The menu — seasonal suggestions, starters, salads, pasta, poultry, and meat —
  was not accessible online, forcing potential guests to call ahead or visit
  before knowing what to expect.
- The private events business had no dedicated online presence, meaning enquiries
  were captured only through a generic email address shared informally through
  word of mouth.
- The all-in menus for events and group bookings — a significant revenue line —
  were not communicated with the clarity needed to convert corporate or
  celebration event planners researching options online.
- The venue serves a bilingual Brussels audience but had no Dutch-language
  presence, limiting its discoverability for Flemish-speaking visitors and
  corporate groups.
- The distinctive setting — wooden terraces over a private pond, an upstairs
  bar-club, a veranda that ranks among the most atmospheric in Brussels —
  was not being communicated visually in a way that drove footfall and reservations.

## My Role

Sole engineer across the full project: discovery, information architecture,
design implementation, front-end and back-end development, bilingual content
system, menu management, online reservation integration, events enquiry system,
deployment, and client handover.

## Solution

### Architecture

Laravel back end powering a Vue.js front end, with all content — menu sections,
seasonal suggestions, event packages, and contact information — managed through
a custom admin panel. The site is fully bilingual in French and Dutch, with
separate URL paths (`/fr/` and `/nl/`) and independently managed translations
for every content area. Photography is stored on AWS S3 and served through
Cloudflare CDN, with the hero and venue imagery treated as a primary conversion
asset throughout the layout.

### Restaurant Menu System

The full restaurant menu is managed through the CMS and presented across six
sections: seasonal suggestions, starters, salads, pasta, poultry, and meat.
Each section is independently editable — the client updates dishes, descriptions,
and prices without developer involvement. Seasonal suggestions are surfaced
prominently as a separate section, reflecting the chef's practice of introducing
market-led specials alongside the core carte.

The Dutch menu (`/nl/onze-kaart/`) mirrors the French menu structure exactly
and is managed as a parallel translation in the admin panel, ensuring both
language versions stay in sync when the menu is updated.

### Online Reservation System

A table reservation flow was integrated directly into the site, giving visitors
a clear path from browsing the menu to booking a table without leaving the
website. The reservation section handles both lunch and dinner covers and is
connected to the venue's internal booking management.

### Events and Private Hire

A dedicated events section communicates The Lodge's private hire and group
catering offer: birthday celebrations, corporate team building, and bespoke
privatisations. All-in menus with per-person pricing are presented as clearly
structured package pages, giving event planners the information they need to
evaluate and enquire without needing a preliminary phone call. A direct event
enquiry form routes requests to the client's events inbox.

### Key Features

- Fully bilingual site in French and Dutch with independent content management
  per language
- Six-section restaurant menu with seasonal suggestions surfaced as a priority
  content area
- Online table reservation system integrated into the site
- Events and private hire section with all-in menu package pages
- Direct event enquiry form for birthday, corporate, and privatisation requests
- Atmospheric venue photography showcasing the pond-side terraces, veranda, and
  bar-club
- Google Maps integration for venue location
- Facebook social integration for the venue's active social community
- Full admin panel for menu, events content, and translation management

## Outcomes

- The Lodge's three revenue activities — restaurant, bar-club, and events — are
  now clearly communicated in a single digital presence that reflects the venue's
  character and quality.
- The online menu gives prospective diners a complete picture of the offering
  before they visit, reducing the barrier to a first reservation particularly
  for new visitors unfamiliar with the venue.
- The events section and all-in menu packages surface the private hire business
  to corporate event planners and celebration organisers who are researching
  venues online — an audience that previously had no dedicated point of contact.
- The Dutch-language version extends the venue's discoverability to
  Flemish-speaking Brussels diners and corporate groups, a segment previously
  unreachable through French-only content.

## Tech Stack Detail

Vue.js handled the more interactive elements — the menu section navigation,
the photo gallery carousel showcasing the venue's setting, and the reservation
flow — while Alpine.js covered lightweight interactions such as the mobile
navigation drawer and the events enquiry form validation. This split kept the
JavaScript payload lean on content-heavy pages like the full menu, where fast
initial load directly affects bounce rate for mobile visitors searching for
Brussels restaurant options.

The bilingual content system was built as a translation layer on top of a
single content model rather than as two separate content trees. Each content
record carries a locale flag, and the admin panel presents both language versions
side by side within the same edit form, making it straightforward for the client
to maintain parity between French and Dutch without navigating between separate
sections.

## Challenges and Learnings

The central design challenge was capturing the atmosphere of The Lodge in a
website layout — the venue's appeal is deeply sensory and experiential, built
around the pond, the wooden terraces, the candlelit veranda, and the upstairs
bar-club in the evening. A purely functional restaurant site with a menu and a
reservation button would have missed what makes The Lodge worth visiting.

The solution was to lead every section with full-width photography that treated
the setting as the primary selling point, and to sequence the homepage so that
a visitor encountered the atmosphere first and the practical information second.
The menu, reservation, and events content was structured for clarity and
conversion; the visual layer was structured for desire.

The events section required particular care in information architecture. The
Lodge handles multiple distinct event formats — birthday dinners, corporate
bookings, full venue privatisations — each with different group sizes, catering
requirements, and decision-maker profiles. Rather than presenting a single
generic events page, the section was structured around these distinct formats
with tailored package descriptions per event type, allowing each audience to
quickly identify the offer that matched their need and move directly to an
enquiry.
